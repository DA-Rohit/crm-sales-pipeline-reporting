# CRM Sales Pipeline Data Management, Analysis & Reporting (Excel, Power Query and Power Pivot)

Excel-based CRM sales pipeline system built using Power Query and Power Pivot for data cleaning, modeling, interactive dashboarding, and generating actionable business insights.

## 📌 Project Overview
- Built an end-to-end Excel-based CRM reporting system  
- Integrated multiple datasets into a structured data model  
- Developed interactive dashboards for performance monitoring  
- Extracted key business insights from sales data

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- Microsoft Excel  
- Power Query  
- Power Pivot  
- Pivot Tables & Charts

## 📂 Dataset
- Sales Pipeline (fact table)  
- Accounts  
- Products  
- Sales Teams

## ⚙️ Key Features
- Data cleaning & standardization (Power Query)  
- Relational data modeling (Power Pivot)  
- KPI development (Revenue, Conversion Rate, Avg Deal Size, Sales Cycle)  
- Interactive dashboards with slicers  
- Performance analysis across agents, products, and time

## 🔍 Key Insights

### 1️⃣ Performance vs Efficiency
We often assume higher revenue comes with longer sales cycles — but the data suggests otherwise:
- Insight: Sales performance shows a directional relationship with deal velocity, where several high-revenue agents close deals faster, while a majority of low-performing agents exhibit longer sales cycles.
- Recommendation: Improving deal velocity through stronger follow-ups and pipeline management may help underperforming agents increase output.

### 2️⃣ Time-based Trend
- Insight: A consistent repeating pattern is observed in both revenue and conversion rate trend line over time, with performance peaking at regular intervals (March, June, September, December) followed by predictable declines, indicating periodic fluctuations in sales performance. Average of conversion rate at peaks and bottoms is 80.6% and 49% respectively. Around 30% difference.
- Recommendation: Sales efforts and pipeline planning should be aligned with these cyclical peaks by increasing deal generation and follow-ups in the preceding months to maximize conversions during high-performance periods.

### Product Mix Impact on Sales Performance

#### 3️⃣ High Volume, Low Value Performance 
- Insight: Despite achieving third highest number of won deals among 6 managers, Dustin’s team generates the lowest revenue due to a heavy concentration on low-priced products, resulting in the lowest average deal size also.
- Recommendation: Shifting focus towards higher-value products or improving upselling strategies within existing deals could significantly enhance revenue without increasing deal volume, allowing the team to convert strong pipeline activity into higher-value outcomes.

#### 4️⃣ Balanced Product Mix Driving High Revenue
- Insight: Melvin’s team leads in revenue and won deals by maintaining a balanced product mix, achieving strong deal volume across mid-to-high value products despite slightly lower conversion rates in two segments.
- Recommendation: Replicating Melvin’s diversified product approach across other teams, especially focusing on mid-to-high value products - can improve overall revenue without relying solely on higher deal counts.

#### 5️⃣ Untapped High-Value Product Opportunity
- Insight: Only Celia’s team has successfully closed deals for the highest-priced product GTK 500, indicating a unique capability to sell premium offerings that remains untapped across other teams.
- Recommendation: Expanding training and enablement around selling high-value products across other teams could unlock significant revenue potential by reducing reliance on lower-priced product volumes.
